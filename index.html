A little about me...

Born and raised in Milwaukee, WI, I graduated from Franklin High School with the class of 2009. I continued my education at the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee where I pursued two majors, Linguistics and Japanese. While at Unviversity, I participated on a student exchange program at Nagasaki Siebold University for one year. After returning to finish my degrees, I graduated from university in May of 2014. That summer in August, I left America to teach English in Japan as an Assistant Language teacher in Japan. In August of 2017, I returned to Milwaukee, WI. Three months later in November, I found work at NPR of America, a Japanese piston ring production company. I started as an Interpreter / Translator, but moved into an IT role several months after I started. In March of 2020, I left NPR for an opportunity at a Japanese software company in Torrance, CA where my wife and I currently live.
